# Luganda seed content and audio notes

## What the seed now includes

- Two Learning Hub stages: greetings and thanks, then family and home.
- Six Learning Game levels. Each stage starts with two small word groups and
  ends with one combined review level.
- Ten Word Game levels: eight core words plus `Awaka` and `Abaana`, which are
  already used in the seeded home story and counting activity.
- Two Counting Game stages: 1â€“3 first, then 1â€“5.
- Ten matching-card concepts and five picture puzzles.
- Two short stories and the existing coloring activities.

The extra levels repeat known words in smaller groups. They do not add a new
grammar topic or claim that a child has mastered Luganda.

## Audio needed before a production release

The generated audio files under `assets/audio/learning/lg` are zero-byte
tracking files. At runtime, Learning Hub keys currently resolve to the shared
`Bulungi.mp3` learning cue, and some standalone games use existing word audio
or the generic `select.mp3` cue. These are development placeholders, not the
pronunciation for the displayed text.

Record and approve these 22 seed-specific clips:

| Group | Clips needed |
| --- | --- |
| Stage 1 phrases | `Oli otya?`, `Gyendi`, `Gyebale ko`, `Webale` |
| Stage 1 story | One clip for each of the 3 story pages |
| Stage 2 words | `Maama`, `Taata`, `Omwana`, `Ennyumba`, `Amazzi`, `Ekitabo` |
| Stage 2 story | One clip for each of the 4 story pages |
| Counting | `Emu`, `Bbiri`, `Ssatu`, `Nnya`, `Ttaano` |

Each final clip should:

- match the approved on-screen Luganda exactly;
- use one clear native speaker in a quiet recording;
- contain no music, English introduction, or sound effect;
- be checked for natural pronunciation and child-friendly pacing;
- replace its placeholder registration in `lib/audioAssets.ts` or the
  standalone game audio map, as appropriate.

The broader content-lead handover may require more recordings than these 22.
See `curriculum_guide/Baby_Steps_Content_Lead_Simple_Guide.md` for global app
instructions and content outside this seed.

## Audio not needed for these extra levels

- Do not record a second copy of a word just because it appears in a small
  practice level and again in the review level. Reuse the approved concept clip.
- The Word Game, matching cards, puzzles, menu cards, and coloring cards do not
  need new spoken clips for this seed.
- `Awaka` and `Abaana` do not need recordings yet because their new use is in
  the spelling-only Word Game. Add recordings if they later become listen or
  pronunciation activities.
- Do not record currency or grouped-counting phrases; those activities are not
  included in this pilot.
- Existing success, error, tap, and background sounds can remain as interface
  sounds. They must not be presented as Luganda pronunciation.

## Images and review still needed

No new image file is required for the added development levels: repeated
levels reuse the concept image, and the two extra puzzles reuse the existing
`Maama` and `Ekitabo` concept images. The tracked Luganda images are still
placeholders and need child-safe, culturally suitable final artwork plus usage
rights before release.

A native Luganda reviewer and an early-years educator must approve the final
words, story sentences, questions, distractors, audio, and images. Until those
checks are complete, keep the readiness/review warnings in metadata even though
the child-facing descriptions are intentionally short.
