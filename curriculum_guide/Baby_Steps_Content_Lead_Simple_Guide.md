**BABY STEPS**

# What the Luganda Content Lead Must Provide

*Simple guide for the Stages 1-2 pilot*

> Your role is to make sure every Luganda word, sentence, story, question, audio recording and image is correct, natural, suitable for children and culturally respectful.

## 1. What you are responsible for

- Review every Luganda word and sentence. Approve it or replace it with the correct version.
- Give the English meaning for every approved Luganda line.
- Ask another bilingual reviewer to translate the final Luganda back into English, so the meaning can be checked.
- Explain when a phrase is used, including whether it is informal, polite, respectful or formal.
- Check that stories, behaviour, clothing, homes and cultural practices are presented accurately and respectfully.
- Approve the final audio after listening to the edited recording.
- Approve the images and confirm that Baby Steps has permission to use them.

## 3. What each type of content should contain

**Text:** Final Luganda wording, English meaning, when it is used, whether it is informal or respectful, and the correct answer where it is a question.

**Audio:** A clear native-speaker recording that matches the approved text exactly. Record in a quiet room, without music or an English introduction.

**Image:** A clear, child-safe and culturally suitable picture. It should not contain written words. The creator, source and permission to use it must be recorded.

**Story:** A short title, three to five short pages, dialogue, a reflection question, one recording and one image per page.

**Quiz:** A simple question, two to four answer choices, one clearly correct answer and no confusing choices.

## 4. Content areas to cover

- Greetings, introductions, thanking, apologising, saying goodbye and other polite language.
- The difference between speaking to a friend and speaking respectfully to an elder, parent, teacher or visitor.
- Body parts and simple feelings such as happy, sad, tired and afraid.
- Cultural practices and behaviour through stories. For example, kneeling when greeting or receiving something should be explained with context and should not be presented as something every family must do.
- Positive behaviour such as sharing, helping, listening, welcoming visitors and asking permission.
- Deeper language in later lessons, including singular and plural words, common sentence patterns, noun agreement and suitable formal and informal expressions.

1. Any unresolved item must remain unchecked and must not be added to the app.
